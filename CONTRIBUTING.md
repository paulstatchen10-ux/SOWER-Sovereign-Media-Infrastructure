# Contributing to SOWER

> *"Two are better than one, because they have a good return for their labor."* — Ecclesiastes 4:9

---

## Who We're Looking For

SOWER is a civic infrastructure project. We're not looking for growth hackers or engagement optimizers. We're looking for:

- Systems architects who care about sovereignty
- Researchers working on neural video compression or codec design
- Civic technologists building for public institutions
- Municipal IT staff interested in pilot node deployment
- Anyone who thinks the internet should belong to its neighbors

---

## Design Constraints Are Non-Negotiable

Before contributing, understand that these are locked:

1. **No tokenization** — do not propose token incentive layers, crypto economics, or convertible reward systems
2. **Energy transparency** — all node implementations must expose power draw metrics
3. **Minimal by default** — do not propose algorithmic feeds or engagement features as defaults
4. **Open API** — no AI vendor lock-in, ever
5. **Municipal backbone** — design decisions must remain viable for civic institution deployment

If a contribution conflicts with these constraints, it will not be merged regardless of technical quality.

---

## How to Contribute

### Research Contributions
Open an Issue labeled `research` with your findings. ZERA codec research, energy accounting models, and federation protocol analysis are highest priority right now.

### Code Contributions
1. Fork the repository
2. Create a branch: `git checkout -b feature/your-feature-name`
3. Make your changes
4. Open a Pull Request with a clear description of what you built and why

### Documentation Contributions
Corrections, expansions, and translations are welcome. Open a PR directly.

### Node Pilot Proposals
If you represent a library, school, university, or municipal institution interested in running a SOWER node, open an Issue labeled `node-pilot` and describe your hardware and institutional context.

---

## AI Collaboration Policy

AI-assisted contributions are welcome and encouraged. If AI tools assisted your contribution, note which ones in your PR description. We practice what we preach — credit where credit is due.
